Cutting-Edge Automotive Technology

Carbon Fiber Innovation

Forged Composite Technology

Lamborghini has pioneered the use of carbon fiber reinforced polymer (CFRP) in automotive applications. The patented Forged Composite technology allows for complex shapes to be created with carbon fiber, reducing weight while increasing strength and rigidity.

This technology was first introduced in the Sesto Elemento and is now used throughout Lamborghini's lineup, including in the monocoque chassis of the Aventador and Revuelto.

Key Benefit: The Aventador's carbon fiber monocoque weighs just 147.5 kg (325 lbs) while offering exceptional rigidity and safety.

Aerodynamics

ALA (Aerodinamica Lamborghini Attiva)

Lamborghini's active aerodynamics system uses electronically controlled flaps and channels to optimize downforce and reduce drag depending on driving conditions. The system can:

  • Increase downforce by up to 750% when needed for cornering
  • Reduce drag by 20% for straight-line speed
  • Activate air brakes for improved stopping power

Powertrain Technology

Hybrid V12 Architecture

The Revuelto represents Lamborghini's first hybrid production car, featuring:

  • 6.5L naturally aspirated V12 engine (814 hp)
  • Three electric motors (combined 218 hp)
  • Total system output of 1,015 hp
  • All-wheel drive with torque vectoring
  • 7-speed dual-clutch transmission

Innovation: The system can operate in pure electric mode for up to 10 km (6 miles), while maintaining the emotional sound and performance of Lamborghini's legendary V12.

Vehicle Dynamics

LDVI (Lamborghini Dinamica Veicolo Integrata)

This integrated vehicle dynamics system acts as the "brain" of modern Lamborghinis, coordinating:

  • All-wheel drive torque distribution
  • Active aerodynamics
  • Four-wheel steering
  • Magnetic suspension damping
  • Stability control systems

The system processes data from multiple sensors at up to 1,000 times per second to optimize vehicle behavior for any driving situation.

Infotainment & Connectivity

Human-Machine Interface

Lamborghini's latest infotainment systems combine cutting-edge technology with driver-focused design:

  • 12.3" digital instrument cluster with multiple display modes
  • 8.4" or 12.3" touchscreen (depending on model)
  • Advanced voice recognition
  • Over-the-air software updates
  • Integrated telemetry systems for track driving

The system maintains Lamborghini's focus on the driving experience while providing modern connectivity features.
