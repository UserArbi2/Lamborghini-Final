About Lamborghini

Our Legacy

Founded in 1963 by Ferruccio Lamborghini, the brand was born from a desire to challenge Ferrari with grand tourers that combined power, luxury, and innovation.

Brand Philosophy

  • Radical Design: From the Miura's curves to the Countach's wedges.
  • Naturally Aspirated Engines: The roar of a V12 is our signature.
  • Sustainability: Hybrid tech meets Italian passion.

Lamborghini in Pop Culture

The unmistakable silhouette of Lamborghini has been featured in countless movies like Fast & Furious, The Dark Knight, and even video games such as GTA V, cementing its status as an icon of automotive excellence and aspirational luxury.

Lamborghini History

1. Founding and Early Success (1963-1972)
o Lamborghini was founded in 1963 by Ferruccio Lamborghini in Sant'Agata Bolognese, Italy, to compete with Ferrari.
o The first models, including the 350GT, were noted for refinement, power, and comfort.
o The Miura (1966) set the standard for mid-engine, rear-wheel-drive sports cars.

2. Financial Troubles and Ownership Changes (1973-1986)
o The 1973 oil crisis led to declining sales, causing Ferruccio Lamborghini to sell the company.
o The company went bankrupt in 1978 and was later bought by Swiss brothers Jean-Claude and Patrick Mimran.
o Under Mimran's leadership, the company expanded its model lineup, introducing cars like the Countach and Jalpa.

3. Chrysler Ownership (1987-1993)
o Chrysler acquired Lamborghini in 1987 and introduced the Diablo (1990), replacing the Countach.
o Lamborghini ventured into Formula 1 with the development of V12 engines but struggled financially.

4. Indonesian and Malaysian Ownership (1994-1997)
o The company was sold to the Indonesian conglomerate MegaTech and later to Mycom Setdco and V'Power Corporation.
o Efforts were made to expand production, but financial difficulties persisted.

5. Volkswagen Group / Audi Ownership (1998-Present)
o Audi acquired Lamborghini in 1998, providing stability and technical advancements.
o New models like the Murciélago (2001) and Gallardo (2003) helped rejuvenate the brand.
o Lamborghini expanded its lineup with high-performance models, including the Reventón (2007), Aventador (2011), Huracán (2014), and Urus (2017).
o The company introduced hybrid technology with the Sián FKP 37 (2019) and announced a hybrid replacement for the Aventador in 2021.

Innovative Technology

Carbon Fiber Revolution

Lamborghini's patented Forged Composite technology creates ultra-light yet rigid chassis structures, first pioneered in the Sesto Elemento.

Active Aero Dynamics

The ALA 2.0 system (Aerodinamica Lamborghini Attiva) automatically adjusts aerodynamics for maximum downforce or minimum drag.

Hybrid V12 Power

The Revuelto combines a 6.5L V12 with three electric motors for 1,001 HP while reducing emissions by 30%.

LDVI Brain

The Lamborghini Dinamica Veicolo Integrata system processes 1,000 data points/second to optimize performance.
