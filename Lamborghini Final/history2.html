The Legend of Lamborghini

The Origins

Ferruccio Lamborghini's Vision

Automobili Lamborghini was founded in 1963 by Ferruccio Lamborghini, a successful tractor manufacturer who wanted to create refined grand touring cars to compete with established brands like Ferrari. The company's headquarters were established in Sant'Agata Bolognese, where they remain today.

Ferruccio Lamborghini was born in 1916 in Renazzo di Cento, Italy. After serving as a mechanic in the Italian Air Force during World War II, he started a business building tractors from surplus military hardware. His tractor company, Lamborghini Trattori, became very successful, allowing him to pursue his passion for sports cars.

The legend says that Lamborghini founded his car company after a disagreement with Enzo Ferrari about the quality of Ferrari's cars. Whether true or not, Lamborghini set out to create cars that combined high performance with superior refinement and comfort.

Lamborghini Timeline

1963: Company Founded

Automobili Lamborghini is officially founded on May 7, 1963. The first model, the 350 GTV prototype, is unveiled at the Turin Motor Show later that year.

1964: 350 GT Production

The production version of the 350 GT is launched, featuring a 3.5L V12 engine producing 270 hp. Only 120 units were built, establishing Lamborghini as a serious competitor in the GT market.

1966: Miura Revolution

The groundbreaking Miura is introduced, featuring a transverse mid-mounted V12 engine. Considered the first true supercar, it set the template for high-performance sports cars for decades to come.

1971: Countach Debut

The Countach LP500 prototype stuns the world with its radical wedge design and scissor doors. The production version would become one of the most iconic supercars of all time.

1973: Financial Troubles

The oil crisis hits the luxury car market hard. Ferruccio Lamborghini sells his remaining shares in the company and retires to his vineyard.

1987: Chrysler Era

American automaker Chrysler acquires Lamborghini, leading to development of the Diablo as the Countach's successor.

1998: Audi Acquisition

The Volkswagen Group, through its subsidiary Audi, purchases Lamborghini, bringing much-needed stability and technical resources to the brand.

2001: Murciélago

The Murciélago debuts as Lamborghini's new flagship, featuring a 6.2L V12 engine and all-wheel drive.

2011: Aventador

The Aventador LP700-4 is introduced with a carbon fiber monocoque chassis and a new 6.5L V12 engine producing 700 hp.

2017: Urus SUV

Lamborghini enters the SUV market with the Urus, combining supercar performance with everyday practicality.

2023: Revuelto

The Revuelto is unveiled as Lamborghini's first hybrid supercar, featuring a 6.5L V12 paired with three electric motors.

Evolution Through Decades

1960s: Establishing the Brand

The 1960s saw Lamborghini establish itself with elegant grand tourers like the 350 GT and 400 GT, before shocking the world with the mid-engine Miura in 1966.

1970s: The Countach Era

The 1970s were dominated by the outrageous Countach, which became the poster car for an entire generation. Despite financial difficulties, Lamborghini continued to innovate with models like the Urraco and Silhouette.

1980s: Survival and the Jalpa

The 1980s were challenging, but Lamborghini survived with the Countach evolution and the more affordable V8-powered Jalpa. The company changed hands several times during this turbulent decade.

1990s: The Diablo

The Diablo carried Lamborghini through the 1990s, with increasingly powerful variants keeping the brand relevant until the Audi acquisition in 1998.

2000s: Modern Renaissance

Under Audi ownership, Lamborghini flourished with the Murciélago and Gallardo, combining traditional Lamborghini drama with German engineering precision.

2010s-Present: Expansion

The current era has seen Lamborghini expand its lineup with the Huracán, Urus SUV, and hybrid technology, while maintaining its position at the pinnacle of supercar performance.

Racing Heritage

While Lamborghini has never officially competed in Formula 1 as a constructor, the company has a rich motorsport history:

  • 1960s: Privateer teams raced modified 350 GT and Miura models
  • 1980s: Lamborghini developed engines for Formula 1 (used by Larrousse, Lotus, and others)
  • 1990s: The Diablo Supertrophy one-make racing series
  • 2000s-present: Successful GT racing programs with the Gallardo and Huracán
  • 2022: Lamborghini enters LMDh prototype racing with the SC63

Today, Lamborghini's Squadra Corse division oversees all motorsport activities, including customer racing programs and the development of track-only models like the Essenza SCV12.
